[/world/rest-of-world/Who-are-Al-Shabaab/articleshow/46787844.cms] * New Shebab attack in northeast Kenya kills 4 [/world/rest-of-world/New-Shebab-attack-in-northeast-Kenya-kills-four/articleshow/46608000.cms] * Obama to finally visit fatherland Kenya as prez [/world/us/Obama-to-finally-visit-fatherland-Kenya-as-president/articleshow/46764970.cms] GARISSA (Kenya): At least 70 Kenyan students were massacred on Thursday when Somalia's Shabaab Islamist group raided a university, the interior minister said, the country's deadliest attack since US embassy bombings in 1998. "We are mopping up the area," Interior minister Joseph Nkaiserry told reporters, saying that four gunmen had been killed after Kenyan troops launched an assault on the final building where the insurgents had holed up for over 12 hours. "Unfortunately, we lost... a number of lives, we have not confirmed fully, but it is in the region of 70 students, and 79 have been injured, nine of them critically," he added. READ ALSO: Islamists seize Christian hostages in Kenya university attack; at least 15 killed [http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/world/rest-of-world/Islamists-seize-Christian-hostages-in-Kenya-university-attack-at-least-15-killed/articleshow/46785038.cms] The masked gunmen began the assault before dawn, using grenades to blast open the gates of the university in the northeastern town of Garissa, near the lawless border with war-torn Somalia, before attacking students as they slept. "The terrorists, 90 per cent of the threat has been eliminated... we have been able to confirm that four terrorists have been killed," he added, saying that troops were scouring the campus as the total number of gunmen was not known, but that the main operation was over. "We are mopping up the area, and will update with the number of casualties," he said, imposing a dawn until dusk curfew on several northern and eastern Kenyan districts. Kenyan police officers take positions outside the Garissa University College as an ambulance carrying the injured going to a hospital, during an attack by gunmen in Garissa in Kenya on Thursday, April 2, 2015. (AP Photo) The al-Qaida-linked Shabaab claimed the pre-dawn attack, the same insurgents who carried out the Westgate shopping mall massacre in Nairobi in September 2013, when four gunmen slaughtered at least 67 people in a four-day bloodbath.
